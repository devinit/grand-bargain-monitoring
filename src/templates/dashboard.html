In the Grand Bargain donors and aid organisations committed to strengthening the transparency of the humanitarian system. As a first step, they agreed to publish timely, transparent, harmonized and open high-quality data on humanitarian funding within two years of the 2016 World Humanitarian Summit and identified the International Aid Transparency Initiative (IATI) Standard as the best way to do this.

The Grand Bargain transparency dashboard is a public online tool, developed by Development Initiatives (DI). It enables Grand Bargain signatories to:

  1. Check the quality of their humanitarian data published to the International Aid Transparency Initiative (IATI) Standard and identify areas requiring improvement.
  2. Assess their progress in meeting the commitment to publish timely, high-quality, harmonised and transparent data on humanitarian funding.

This page shows the date when the organisation first published to IATI and measures the progress they are making in meeting the Grand Bargain transparency commitment according to the monitoring methodology (see methodology tab). Any value greater than zero is coloured green and shows that an organisation’s data quality has improved compared with their Baseline value as of 1 June 2017 (in most cases). The higher the figure the more progress an organisation is making at that given point in time.

For details on how these values are calculated hover over column headings for a brief description or see the methodology tab.

The monitoring framework and methodology

The monitoring framework and methodology explained below was developed by Development Initiatives with the support of the IATI Secretariat following an extensive consultation process, which was carried out during 2017 and involved over 150 people from 85 organisations across 26 countries.

What is being measured?

The methodology measures the quality of humanitarian data an organisation publishes to the IATI Standard against five performance measures. Each of these performance measures has been given a percentage weighting that reflects its importance and/or relevance for the humanitarian community, based on feedback received through the consultation process. The purpose, weighting and methodology for each performance measure is outlined in the table below. More details on the methodologies for each performance measure can be found on the IATI website by clicking on the links below.

Performance measure Purpose Weighting Methodology
1. Humanitarian To assess the use of the specific humanitarian elements added to the IATI Standard at v2.02 25% Methodology
2. Timeliness To assess both the frequency (how often the data is updated) and the time lag (how up to date the data is) of published information 25% Methodology
3. Comprehensiveness To assess how much of the IATI Standard is being used 25% Methodology
4. Coverage To assess the percentage of an organisation's total operational spend on all humanitarian programming that is published to IATI 15% Methodology Additional information will be required from publishers (see above)
5. Forward looking To assess how much information on activity budgets is available for the next three years 10% Methodology

What is classified as humanitarian data?

An organisation’s data is classed as humanitarian if either of the following applies:

  • The activity includes a DAC sector code in the range 72010 to 74010.
  • The activity humanitarian attribute is set. (Note that the humanitarian attribute is only available to publishers using V2.02 or later of the IATI Standard.)

Where is the data taken from and how often is it updated?

The Grand Bargain Transparency Dashboard uses the data that organisations publish to the IATI Standard on the IATI Registry. The dashboard is updated on a daily basis and the values under each column are calculated automatically.

Making progress towards meeting the transparency commitments

An organisation's overall assessment is based on its total value (see Details tab) according to the quintile ratings outlined in the table below. For example, an organisation with a total value 41.5% would receive an assessment of ‘fair”. Organisations achieving an assessment of “good” (60–79%) or above have met the Grand Bargain transparency commitment to publish timely, high-quality, harmonised and transparent data on humanitarian funding.

Quintile Assessment
80% to 100% Very good
60% to 79% Good
40% to 59% Fair
20% to 39% Poor
0 to 19% Very poor

The specific case of federated/confederated organisations

The operational models of some organisations with highly federated or confederated structures can present significant challenges for implementing organisation-wide IATI publishing. National members or affiliates often have independent governance structures and are subject to differing legal requirements depending on national laws. This presents difficulties when measuring their performance and presenting an aggregated value for the federation/confederation. Therefore all Grand Bargain signatory organisations, including any national members or affiliated organisations that publish their humanitarian aid data to IATI, will be individually assessed and these values made available on the Grand Bargain Transparency Dashboard.
